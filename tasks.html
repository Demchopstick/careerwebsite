Tasks and Duties

There are many types of Video Game Designers
Here is the 4 Main Types

Content Video Game Designer

A Content Game Designer focuses on creating specific elements and experiences within a game after the core mechanics and systems are already in place. This includes designing quests, dialogue, encounters, puzzles, and other content that players interact with.

Level Video Game Designer

Level designers in video game development are responsible for creating the layout, challenges, and overall experience of game levels, often referred to as the "stages" of the game. They combine art, systems, and gameplay to create engaging and immersive spaces for the player to interact with.

Systems Video Game Designer

System video game designers focus on the mechanics, rules, and systems that drive gameplay, but are not directly controlled by the player. They are responsible for creating the foundation of the game experience, including progression, balance, and resource management.

Experience Video Game Designer

Experienced game designers are responsible for creating the overall experience and mechanics of a game, from conceptualizing the core idea to implementing the final product
